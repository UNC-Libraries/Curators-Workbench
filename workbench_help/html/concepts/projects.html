Projects

Materials in the workbench are divided into projects within the work space. Depending on the size of a collection, the level or levels of granularity with which you plan to treat the collection, or other considerations, you may decide to use one or many projects to capture a collection.

When deciding whether a collection will treated by one overarching project, or divided into a series of smaller projects, it is helpful to consider that the workbench generates a METS manifest file for each project. Larger collections may benefit from being chunked into smaller parts, for instance, an expansive, heterogeneous collection may be divided into more homogeneous units.

Finally, while it is possible to work on many projects at once, this may slow down workbench performance. By closing projects you will free up system resources, while retaining work completed within the closed project.
